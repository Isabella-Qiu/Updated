Exploring Autodesk Maya

Download Project File



Sepember 2021 - Video Game Art & Design

This school year, I started learning how to model 3D objects using Autodesk Maya. Currently, I am still exploring the basics of Maya, having no previous experience with 3D graphics applications, but I am tremendously excited to practice and become better at modeling more complex objects. Shown is one of my most recent projects. I attempted to model a flower, and used various reference photos to correctly shape flower petals and assign realistic colors. The shape itself is fairly complex for what I was familiar with, but I realized that if I could split a complex object into a simpler one, the modeling process would be much easier. The biggest obstacle of this project was the flower portion. I used one Polygon Plane for each petal, and heavily utilized the Soft-Select feature, which allowed me to mold each petal with a proportionate falloff radius. This made the final product look much smoother.









Bridge Designer Project

March 2021 - Principles of Engineering

By utilizing the West Point Bridge Designer Software, my partner and I were able to design a stable, inexpensive bridge capable of crossing the Trinity River. The constraints of this project forced us to think as real-life bridge designers, taking into account cost of bridge material, structural stability, as well as the effects of tension, compression, and dynamically applied force.





Compound Machine Project

Link to Documentation



December 2020 - Principles of Engineering

We thought that pouring milk into cereal was too boring, so we created a compound machine for a more fun and complicated way to pour milk into cereal. In order to arrive at a final mechanism, we had to follow through with PLTW's Design Process, and our prototype needed to include at least 3 mechanisms per person in our team, as well as applied effort force that may only be provided by a single human input. All our materials were also limited to be common household items. Given the restraints and time window we had to complete this project, my teammates and I had to meticulously plan our time and record our own individual modifications and design processes. We found that some had more luck than others when building, but we all went through many rounds of design and testing to arrive at a final compound machine.
